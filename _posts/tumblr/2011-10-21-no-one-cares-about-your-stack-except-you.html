Growing up, my dad always had these Far Side comics on his desk. Some were over my head, but the ones that always stuck out to me we’re the ones about Macs vs ‘PC’s vs Linux. 

People would get really heated about which operating system was better, how the other one was inferior, and how anybody who used one of the other ones was just “too stupid” to get it. I watched countless real arguments crop up over this stuff. 

We were a windows family, so I grew up a 'PC’ guy. I didn’t have a Mac, and as such I never really used one. I learned the windows paradigm. Without knowing, I started to think that it was the “right” way. For sometime, I equated Macs to over priced toasters*.

When I did eventually find myself on a Mac, everything seemed out of place. What do you mean I can’t delete that file?! How do I get to DOS?  Where are all the programs I use?!

Of course, this led to frustration… which led me to believe one was better than the other… which led to the comics I used to see on my dads desk when I was younger.

My dad (even though he’ll admit he sometimes would flame out in a Mac vs Windows vs Linux argument from time to time) finally offered some sage advice :

The best program is the one you know

It makes perfect sense. And it’s all coming back full circle for me.

I’ve spent the last few (6?!) years learning as much as I can about the web and the technology that powers it. I started by trying to build a basic web page for a business I was building in college my freshman year. I had 0 knowledge on where to even start, so I just started googling tutorials. Eventually, I bought a book on HTML, PHP and MySQL. 

As I learned more, I learned how little I actually knew. I kept diving deeper and deeper, only to find that there was yet another level I didn’t fully grok. It was intimidating.

Then I started to hear about ruby on rails, django/python. Was I taking the right path? Was PHP really an amateaur language? 

Suddenly I started to read posts about how PHP was an inferior language. That is wasn’t true OOP. That ruby had a simpler syntax. That python was more flexible.

When I spoke about what stack I used, I always prefaced PHP with “I’m learning rails but right now I’m using PHP”. To be honest, I had a programmer complex - I felt impotent because I used the “noob" language.

But when I dove into using rails, I had to start over. How do I send mail? Where is all the support? Where are all my libraries? Suddenly simple things I could just "do” in PHP I needed to re learn in rails. 

Fortunately, I’ve come to realize that no one cares. I just want to build the product in the best way that I can. Our users don’t even know what language we’re using, so why does it matter? The cool sexy languages are great- and I’m happy to learn about them -but the bottom line is this:

The best language is the one you know.

*for the record, I love my Mac. I use all three operating systems in one environment with an awesome program called synergy
