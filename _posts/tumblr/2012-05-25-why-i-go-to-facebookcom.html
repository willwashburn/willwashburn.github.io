This could just as easily be titled “Why I don’t go to Facebook.com as much anymore”, but let’s stick to the point. Here are some reasons I still visit Facebook and some commentary on what I think it means for Facebook’s future.

Keeping up with Facebook drama is like a Soap Opera for me. I can’t wait to see what happens in next week’s episode!!

-Cory Bridges, Facebook friend of Will Washburn

Judging people I don’t really care about
Who is dating who now? Did SHE have a baby with HIM? Oh snap, she really got fat. Damn, he got in shape. Wait, who is this again? Wow, they really are living the life.

Most of my news feed is filled with people I sort of knew, never really knew, or who have left my life in one form or another. Is my life more enriched seeing their life stories? I’m not sure, but I know I am curious to see who does what and where they end up. Just not curious enough to pick up the phone, and you know, actually call them. So, Facebook has become a place I go to to judge people I used to know. Sad, true, and honest.

Who is this person?
Wait, Stephanie who? Oh the blonde? No, Jeff’s girlfriend? Wait, who? OOOOOHHH her.

If I need to see a picture of someone, I go on Facebook and pretty quickly find a picture of them. It’s the easiest way to “see” someone. Writing this makes it sound really creepy, but I’m sure I’m not the only one who does this.

Messaging a normal person (whose email I don’t have)
If I don’t have their number or email and they don’t have twitter, I’ll use Facebook to send a message to someone. It beats searching anywhere else, even though Facebook’s messaging is so bad I’m not sure they even get it.

Seeing photos of _________ event that I was at
If I’ve been tagged, I want to see the photos. If there was an event, I want to see if I was in one of the photos taken at the event. I’m sounding pretty narcissistic, but I’m focusing on being honest here. Give me a break.

Notable Things Not On This List

  1. Connecting with brands 
  2. Connecting with close friends
  3. Sharing pictures with family
  4. Finding interesting articles
  5. Figuring out what a company does / “who” a company is
  6. When I’m looking to buy anything
Facebook still has (for me) some ground to cover if they want to be the source of information in these areas. Facebook has become a social directory, but in a much more public way than would allow some of these things (ergo I’d feel weird sharing “family photos”). It hasn’t exactly become a corporate brand directory though, even though I think they’ve tried. With that in mind, I don’t think of brands on Facebook and generally ignore them. Sharing links and articles is not the reason I go to Facebook, although I’m sure some people use it for that.
 
I find that my uses are generally unhealthy/negative, so I don’t have the same positive feelings I used to have for it. In the beginning, it was fun and exciting - just my close friends from school. It was positive. We shared photos and stories and posted jokes on each other’s walls.
 
I’m not sure if it’s just because I’ve left that time in my life, but it’s become something much more perverse. Either I don’t use it in the right way or it’s naturally run it’s course. It’s a negative time sink for me, despite what I think it could be or have been.
 
I’m sure I’ll still tune in for next weeks episode though, just like some cheesy reality television show.
