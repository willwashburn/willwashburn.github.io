I’ve never had a beer. I’ve never been drunk, wasted, blacked out, sloshed, plastered, hammered or any other of the many terms for being over-indulgently inebriated.

Yet, time and time again people come up to me and say that, while I probably don’t remember, I was super drunk when they saw me.

Perhaps it’s a defensive maneuver on my part, and I acted that way to stop the inevitable barrage of questions that come from admitting I don’t drink. Perhaps it’s assumed from context, as one who is at a bar at 2am was, more than likely, drunk - especially with all that dancing going on. Perhaps people see what they want and don’t like the idea that someone was sober. Perhaps it’s just conversation.

Whatever the reality is, when people find out I don’t drink they always want to know; why?

It seems incomprehensible that I just choose not to, so people always ask a series of mildly amusing questions. 

Are you super religious?
Are you allergic?! 
Are you a recovering alcoholic?!
Does anyone in your family drink? 
Do you hate fun? SUNSHINE?!
WHY WOULD YOU NOT DRINK, YOU SEEM NORMAL?! 

The real reason is, of course,  a much simpler answer than these types of questions imply. There is no long story, no heart wrenching tale about how I overcame some battle with booze; The truth is that I just would prefer to be sober.

Yes, I prefer sobriety. I like to be sober. (this is where my date gets up and leaves me at dinner -true story). I know what you’re thinking - how can I know?

There is a chance that I don’t know how great it is to be drunk, and that I am really missing out. That sober is this horrible state, and that the haze of liquor is something I would be stupid not to enjoy. Don’t knock it until you’ve tried it, as the saying goes. 

The truth is I don’t mind not drinking and see plenty of reasons not to start.

Hangovers don’t seem fun. Drinks are really expensive, especially in a city like New York. I don’t need the extra calories at 2AM, especially if my sugar addiction stays as constant. I get a kick out of toying with drunk people with my sudden advantage in intellect, one that would disappear if I too were drunk. I have a ton of fun being sober - why would I change that?

How can I go out and still have fun? Most people ask this because whenever they go out sober they are miserable. They point to the alcohol as the reason they can have fun. “Liquid Courage” makes conversation easier and more things happen. I can totally see the correlation.

However, correlation does not mean causation. Attitude can be adjusted. No one needs to drink to have fun. 

Try it. Go to a bar and introduce yourself to someone you don’t know. Dance. Say something you wouldn’t normally say. Play the rejection game. Smile. Act drunk if it makes it easier. Create your own “liquid courage”.

I’m not saying everyone should quit drinking. I don’t think it’s entirely bad and I think for some people, it’s a great shortcut to being social and “loosening up”. 

I’m just suggesting it’s ok to party without the shortcut, too.
