I wrote this post as my jump back into blogging and the “Blog So Hard Challenge”. I seem to have confused some people.

I’m not sad. I’m not a victim. I am certainly not unlucky or suffering from some unfair life disadvantage.

In fact, it’s precisely the opposite. I have had more advantages and loving people around me than 99.9% of the world. I wake up every morning and work on an interesting problem in a way that I see fit. I am healthy and fortunate in every way possible. I’ve been given every opportunity at every stage of my life and been supported no matter if I screwed up or not. 

Said another way, I have nothing to complain about.

My point in that post was a simple one: it’s time to stop pretending. Regardless of my advantages I haven’t found the success that I’ve been seeking and it’s been a bit of an emotional roller-coaster.

You might say I’m being a little sensitive and should just “man up”. I would argue that I am.

I think it’s a shame that admitting emotion is considered weak. I think many people in this industry (and others) struggle with self-doubt and emotional distress, but bottle it up and eventually burn out. 

It’s easier to act tough and pretend it all doesn’t bother you. There is no vulnerability in being above it all. 

I believe it takes someone with real strength to confront their emotions and admit weakness. It is not a cry for help, but rather a statement that

“yeah, this is hard for me.. but I’m still going to make it happen”

There is strength in honesty, especially when the truth is difficult to admit.
