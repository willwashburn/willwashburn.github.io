Are you a Mac or a PC?

Generally speaking, people get pretty protective of the operating system that they use. If there was some sort of allegiance we all make when we first sign on, I think I might have missed it. Why do we get so defensive?

The laptop I’m currently writing on came bundled with Windows 7, but I wiped it for Ubuntu 12.01. I love it. My desktop configuration is an i7 rig (running Windows 7) and iMac (running OSX) combo using synergy. I couldn’t function without them. Suffice to say, I’m using all three OS’s every day. Why doesn’t everyone else?

My Dad recently got into building “Hackintosh"s that use barebone PC components and get OSX running on it. It’s cool stuff for sure, but one of the greatest benefits is being able to triple boot Windows, OSX and some version of Linux on the same machine.

I know most people love Mac hardware, and I agree that it is awesome, but the price just doesn’t   match what you can get building it yourself. Now that you can build themwithout breaking the bank, maybe more people will be able to get into poly-os'n.

It may be out of your comfort zone, but as the instructions on tony mac’s hackintosh site say: 

let go of your fear of your computer

Diversity is good in all other aspects of life, why not in your OS?
